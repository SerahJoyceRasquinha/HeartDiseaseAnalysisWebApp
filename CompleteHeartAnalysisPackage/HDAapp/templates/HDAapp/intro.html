What is Heart Disease?

Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart.
Heart disease is perceived as the deadliest disease in human life across the world.
The heart cannot push the required quantity of blood to the remaining organs.
The most common type is coronary artery disease (CAD), which involves the narrowing or blockage of coronary arteries.

Causes

  • Atherosclerosis: Build-up of fatty deposits (plaque) in the arteries.
  • Hypertension: Increased pressure in arteries.
  • High Cholesterol: Elevated LDL levels and low HDL levels.
  • Smoking: Damages blood vessels and reduces oxygen in the blood.
  • Diabetes: High blood sugar damages blood vessels.
  • Obesity: Excess weight increases risk factors.
  • Genetics: Family history of heart disease.
  • Sedentary Lifestyle: Lack of physical activity.
  • Poor Diet: Diets high in fats and cholesterol.

Symptoms

  • Chest Pain or Discomfort.
  • Shortness of Breath.
  • Fatigue.
  • Palpitations.
  • Swelling in legs, ankles, or feet.
  • Dizziness or Lightheadedness.

Diagnosis

  • Electrocardiogram (ECG): Measures electrical activity of the heart.
  • Echocardiogram: Uses sound waves to create heart images.
  • Stress Test: Measures heart function during exercise.
  • Coronary Angiography: Uses X-rays to check blood flow.
  • Blood Tests: To check cholesterol and blood sugar levels.

Treatment

  • Lifestyle Changes: Diet modification, physical activity, and quitting smoking.
  • Medications: Statins, beta-blockers, ACE inhibitors, and anticoagulants.
  • Procedures:
    • Angioplasty and Stenting.
    • Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG).
    • Implantable Devices like pacemakers.

Prevention

  • Maintain a Healthy Diet.
  • Exercise Regularly.
  • Quit Smoking.
  • Manage Stress.
  • Control Blood Pressure and Cholesterol.
  • Maintain a Healthy Weight.
  • Regular Check-ups.

Recent Trends

Recent studies focus on understanding the genetic predispositions to heart disease, improving non-invasive diagnostic techniques, and enhancing personalized treatment plans. Additionally, wearable health tech and AI-based analysis are playing pivotal roles in early detection and continuous monitoring.
